1. Location and Features:
    Rara Lake lies at an elevation of 2,975 m (9,760 ft) and has a water surface of 10.8 kmÂ² (4.2 sq mi). It is 5.1 km (3.2 mi) long and 2.7 km (1.7 mi) wide, with a maximum depth of 167 m (548 ft). The lake drains into the Mugu Karnali River via the Nijar River. Rara Lake is surrounded by forested hills like Chuchemara Danda and Murma, offering stunning views of the Saipal Mountain from the lake.
  2. Socio-cultural and Religious Significance:
    The area around Rara Lake is mainly inhabited by people engaged in agriculture and goat rearing. The Thakur society is dominant in the community, and Thakur Baba's Temple is located in the southeast corner of the lake. Local beliefs attribute the opening of the lake's passage to the god Thakur, reducing flood damage.
  3. National Park and Wildlife:
    Rara Lake is the main feature of Rara National Park, established in 1976 to preserve the lake's beauty and surrounding flora and fauna. The park is home to rare and vulnerable species, including the endangered red panda, musk deer, Himalayan black bear, snow leopard, and various bird species. The park offers diverse wildlife viewing opportunities and a rich botanical experience.
  4. Activities and Attractions:
    Rara Lake is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering activities like trekking, hiking, fishing, boating, camping, wildlife viewing, and cultural tours. The region provides breathtaking views of mountains, forests, and wildlife, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
